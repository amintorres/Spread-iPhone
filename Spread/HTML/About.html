About

Spread is a marketplace for news and creative photography.

News photos

Spread is platform that allows citizens to capture newsworthy photographs from their mobile devices and make them available to blogs and news organizations for immediate purchase.

Photos are released under two standardized licenses, RFW (Ready For Web) and RFP (Ready For Print). Both are available for anyone to purchase at a flat rate. Magazines, news organizations, ad agencies and individuals can also submit photo requests to reach out to the Spread photographer community. Photographers can then submit photos against these requests, which becomes a pool of freshly made images from where the photo requester can choose.

All photo requests are pre-paid to ensure that the financial transaction between the photo requester and the photographer is immediate.

Advisers

We also have two amazing advisers, Jacob Silberberg and Jez Coulson, who have been a great help in regards to more complex market specific knowledge that Amin and Korbin do not have directly.

Jacob Silberberg , born 1979 in Washington, DC, has worked as a photojournalist and commercial photographer since 2002. He has worked in 40 countries, including photographing wars, conflicts, and disasters in Iraq, Darfur, Liberia, Colombia, Ivory Coast, the Niger Delta, Venezuela, and India, in addition to covering the 2008 presidential campaigns. Silberberg's photo credits include virtually every major publication and new media outlet including The New York Times, TIME, Newsweek, Paris Match, The Guardian, Der Spiegel, GQ, Vanity Fair, Marie Claire and CNN. His work has won dozens of awards in addition to being hung in London's National Portrait Gallery. He was a recipient of a 2006 fellowship for international reporting at Johns Hopkins School of Advanced International Studies.

Jez Coulson is an internationally renowned reportage artist whose photojournalism has won many accolades and has appeared in the world's leading magazines His assignments have taken him across continents: from the frozen coal mines of Siberia to the extreme heat of the Negev desert. From the teeming rain forests of Ghana to the windswept emptiness of the South Atlantic Falkland Islands. He has covered the slums of Bombay and the Place of Westminster. Glamorous parties in Cannes to the horror of war in the Congo. His pictures show joy at the bringing down of the Berlin wall and elation at the election of Nelson Mandela. But also the brutality of ethnic cleansing. Shock and sadness in the aftermath and rubble of the Twin Towers. Devastation around the Gulf in the wake of Hurricane Katrina and the BP oil spill.
